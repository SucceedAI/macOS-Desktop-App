## LinkedIn Founder Post

Today I am launching SucceedAI on Product Hunt.

The problem is simple: AI writing tools are powerful, but they often force you to leave the app where the writing is happening.

That creates a slow loop:
copy text, open another app, prompt AI, copy the response, paste it back, fix the flow.

SucceedAI is built around a different workflow. It lives in the macOS menu bar and lets you type a command directly in any editable text field. Press Return, and the command is replaced with AI-generated text in place.

Use cases:
- rewrite emails
- summarize notes
- draft support replies
- polish product copy
- translate messages
- clean up rough thoughts

I would value feedback from Mac users, writers, founders, support teams, and anyone who uses AI every day but dislikes context switching.

Body:
I built SucceedAI because my personal AI writing workflow had too much friction. For short tasks like rewriting an email, summarizing notes, or polishing a reply, opening a browser chatbot felt heavier than the task itself.

The app lives in the macOS menu bar. You type a command trigger in an editable text field, describe the task, press Return, and the command is replaced with generated text in place.

I am looking for feedback from Mac users who write a lot. The main questions I am trying to answer are:
- Is command-based AI text replacement faster than opening a chat window?
- Which trigger feels natural?

## Reply Bank

Thanks for checking it out. The core idea is to make AI writing feel like a native text action instead of another app to manage.

The app needs Accessibility permission because macOS requires that for global keyboard detection and text insertion. I tried to make that permission flow explicit rather than surprising.

The trigger is configurable from Settings > Keys, so users are not locked into the default.

The current focus is writing workflows: rewrite, summarize, translate, shorten, expand, polish, and draft replies.
